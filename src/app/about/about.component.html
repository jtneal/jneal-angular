About

I began developing websites in the mid-to-late 90's using popular tools of the time such as Geocities, Angelfire, and Expage. Oh, how times have changed, but even after all these years, I still maintain now the same passion for building websites that I had then.

I began my professional experience in 1999 when I joined my high school's web page staff. We worked as a team to keep the website up-to-date and make improvements as necessary. While everyone else was using Microsoft's FrontPage to make their changes, I was quickly noticed for typing my HTML by hand. In 2001, I became the web staff manager. This made me the head student in charge of the website until I graduated in 2003.

In 2006, I received my first full-time job offer in web development. Since that time, I have grown and matured into a highly skilled developer with quite a repertoire of skills and abilities. My primary focus has been the LAMP stack, more specifically Linux, Apache, MySQL and PHP. However, lately I've been enjoying LEMP - Linux, Nginx, MariaDB, PHP-FPM. I prefer Object-Oriented PHP with an MVC architecture. I follow standards and write code that is both clean and secure. I believe it goes without saying that I am very strong with xHTML/CSS and always follow the standards set forth by the W3C.

I analyise the requirements of each project and always use the right tool for the right job. Currently, my preferred setup is to separate all front-end HTML/CSS/JS from the back-end PHP/DB. I do this by writing an API using Silex for the back-end and AngularJS applications on the front-end. I have also built plenty of websites with their own custom content management system. One of my most prized creations is a custom content management system and social network designed specifically for bands, record labels, and other entities in the entertainment industry. Some of the more popular names that have used it are Alien Ant Farm, Egypt Central, 12 Stones, The Fairchilds, and Chris Adler from Lamb of God.

If you learn only one thing about me after visiting my website, I hope it is that I am very passionate about my work. I love developing websites and web applications, and I couldn't imagine having a better job or career. Even after completing a full day of work at my full time gig, I typically find myself at home working on my own projects late into the night. I love learning, I love improving, and that's why I thoroughly enjoy this line of work.
